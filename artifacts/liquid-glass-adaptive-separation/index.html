One concept: adaptive separation

Liquid Glass is not just “blur, but prettier.”

The elegant idea is that the UI becomes a thin, system-rendered material layer that constantly earns legibility: it samples what is behind it, bends/tints/separates as needed, and stays out of the content’s way.

Core insight: transparency is allowed only when hierarchy remains clear.

1. It is optical, not decorative.

Apple describes Liquid Glass as a “digital meta-material” that bends and shapes light, using lensing/refraction cues so floating controls still read as objects.

2. It is adaptive, not fixed.

The interesting part is not one CSS value. Apple says layers continuously adapt to what is behind them: shadows, tint, and dynamic range shift to keep controls legible.

3. It is restrained, not everywhere.

Apple’s own guidance says to reserve it for navigation floating above content and avoid “glass on glass,” because the whole trick collapses when hierarchy gets muddy.
