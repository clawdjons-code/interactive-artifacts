Epicure · navigable food geometry
One concept · food embeddings are maps

A food model is useful when you can steer through it.

Epicure turns 1,790 ingredients into 300-dimensional points. The trick is not “AI recommends recipes.” The trick is a map: nearby ingredients, named regions, and directions you can rotate toward.

Core insight: Co-occurrence answers “what do cooks use together?” Chemistry answers “what shares sensory structure?” A chef-facing tool needs both, plus a steering knob.

3 · Explain

The model is an atlas, not a recipe book.

The paper’s useful abstraction is a set of operators over one ingredient space.

Nearest neighbors

Find what is close to a seed ingredient. Cooc returns companions; Chem returns profile peers.

Mode lookup

Ask which named emergent neighborhood an ingredient belongs to: wok essentials, fermented pantry, sweet confectionery.

ingredient → closest mode

Direction arithmetic

Rotate an ingredient toward a supervised or emergent direction, then retrieve what lives there.

What the tweet image’s F₁₄ modes mean

A factor is not a label. It is a recovered axis. Its high-scoring ingredients split into modes — small neighborhoods with interpretable culinary identity.

M0Chinese savory fermented and umami ingredients: sweet bean sauce, fermented chive flower paste, century egg.
M1Sweet confectionery and dessert bases: chocolate, cream, cookie, syrup, caramel.
M2Chinese wok cooking essentials: light soy sauce, oyster sauce, Shaoxing wine, doubanjiang, Sichuan peppercorn.
M3East Asian stir-fry vegetables and freshwater proteins: scallion, tofu, enoki, wood ear, bok choy.
M4Mediterranean cheese and olive oil staples: olive oil, balsamic vinegar, feta, pecorino, olives.

Sources and caveats

  • Primary source: Radzikowski & Chen, “Epicure: Navigating the Emergent Geometry of Food Ingredient Embeddings,” arXiv:2605.22391v1.
  • Tweet summarized: Josef Chen’s May 26, 2026 post announcing the paper.
  • The ~2 MB claim refers to the core 1,790 × 300 float32 ingredient embedding table, not the full dataset or training pipeline.
  • The paper says code and trained artifacts are not released at this time, so this artifact explains the reported method/results rather than reproducing the model.
Important caveat: “0 labels used” is fair for the unsupervised factor/mode geometry, but the pipeline still uses LLMs for ingredient normalization, cuisine tagging for evaluation, and naming the discovered modes.
