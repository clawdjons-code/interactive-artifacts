Same rule, different machines

Rule A: object beyond f

A converging lens forms a real, inverted image. Move the object closer to f and the image moves farther away and grows.

1/f = 1/do + 1/di

Rule B: object inside f

The outgoing rays spread. Your eye traces them backward and sees a virtual, upright, magnified image — a simple magnifier.

Rule C: diverging lens

A concave lens always spreads rays. The apparent image is virtual, upright, reduced, and on the same side as the object.
